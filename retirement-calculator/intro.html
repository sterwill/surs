Citizens of Illinois, the other Squeezy warns you!

It has come to my attention that a high ranking Illinois politician (who shall remain nameless) has recently appropriated a very unflattering likeness of me to shill for him in a campaign designed to misinform Illinois citizens about a very serious problem facing all of us.

After decades of the State skipping and shortchanging payments to state workers' retirement funds, this politician now calls the situation a “pension problem.” (When I max-out my credit card, I call it what it is – a DEBT problem.) Worse, this politician's twisted logic now blames State workers and retirees (who always paid their full share) for this huge DEBT that past Governors and Legislators have willfully and deliberately created for Illinois taxpayers. Contrary to what that other “Squeezy” says, there is no pension problem, only a huge DEBT owed to State workers and retirees. This DEBT was deliberately foisted on current and future taxpayers by past and current politicians. Now, instead of owning up to the DEBT problem they created, these politicians are attempting to blame “lavish” and “unsustainable” public pensions for this huge DEBT. (It seems they will do most anything to avoid taking responsibility for their own actions!)

Rather than continuing to be a shill for these politicians, I challenge them to prove, with numbers, their claims about “lavish” and “unsustainable” pensions! The truth is, pension reform may be as simple as the State paying its share along with money they take from workers' pay.

In the following pages, I make my case that the Illinois public pension plan I am a member of, is not lavish, is affordable and is a good bargain for employees, retirees and taxpayers. See what you think.
